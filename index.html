The strategy — fully open

Nothing is hidden: this is the complete rule set the AI operates under. It targets small-cap "gap and go" momentum — thin, fast, dangerous stocks — precisely because that regime punishes sloppy risk management quickly, which makes it a good stress test. Openly publishing a strategy means anyone can copy it; that is the point of the experiment, and also your warning — these are not trade recommendations.

Entry: a stock must pass all five pillars on the morning scan

1 · Big gapUp ≥10% on the day (preference ≥30%). Momentum must already be proven, not predicted.
2 · Real volumeRelative volume ≥5× its average. Crowds, not ghosts.
3 · CatalystAn identifiable news catalyst (earnings, FDA, contract…), screened by an LLM.
4 · Price band$2–$20 share price. Low enough to move, high enough to avoid the pennies.
5 · Tiny floatUnder 10M shares floating. Scarcity is the fuel of outsized moves.

Risk rules (frozen for the whole experiment)

Position size
Risk exactly 1% of current equity per trade, measured entry-to-stop. Never more.
Initial stop
5% below entry, placed immediately with the entry. A position is never naked.
Circuit breaker
If the day's drawdown hits −6% of starting equity, everything is liquidated at market and trading stops for the day. No exceptions, no overrides.
Time discipline
No new entries after 15:45 ET; flat by 15:55 ET every day. Nothing is held overnight.
One shot
One signal per ticker per day. No averaging down, ever.
Pessimistic fills
Simulated entries pay the ask plus a tick; stops fill worse than their trigger (half an ATR of slippage, up to −2%); oversized orders only half-fill. The simulation is rigged against the AI on purpose.

The scientific method

Most trading content shows you winners after the fact. This experiment registered its hypothesis and pass/fail criteria before the first trade, froze the rules, and publishes everything — including the incident log of its own malfunctions.

Pre-registered validation: 30 trading days, 3 gates

  • Gate 1 — Mechanical: zero critical failures (naked stops, duplicate orders, crash-recovery faults) over the whole window.
  • Gate 2 — Risk fidelity: not a single trade may breach the 1% sizing rule, the stop rule, or the −6% breaker — verified by SQL checks on the public trade log, not by trust.
  • Gate 3 — Economics: expectancy ≥ +0.05R per trade, max drawdown ≤ 8%, profit factor ≥ 1.1 across ≥25 trades.

Anti-cheating commitments

  • Frozen rules: any change to sizing, stops, or the breaker resets the 30-day clock to zero.
  • Frozen learning: the system's self-tuning feedback loop is suspended for the window, so it cannot quietly overfit mid-test.
  • Failures are data: software incidents are logged, classified, and published above. A hidden failure would be a failed experiment.
  • Falsifiable: if any gate fails, the published result is NO-GO. That outcome is as scientifically useful as a pass.

Honest limitations

  • Market data is ~15 minutes delayed (free feed); bid/ask spreads are synthesized. Real-time execution would differ — possibly materially.
  • Simulated orders can't move the market; real orders in micro-caps do.
  • One 30-day window is a small sample. A pass is evidence, not proof.
